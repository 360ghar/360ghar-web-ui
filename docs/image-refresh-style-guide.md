# 360Ghar Image Refresh Style Guide

## Purpose
This brief standardizes replacement assets so the website feels India-first, Gurugram/NCR-first, and materially more credible without changing any layout or brand styling.

## Core Direction
- Geography: Prefer Gurugram and Delhi NCR for hero, property, and locality imagery. Use broader Indian contexts for support and editorial slots.
- People: Cast Indian buyers, owners, renters, advisors, and support staff with natural skin tones and believable age ranges.
- Mood: Calm daylight, aspirational but grounded, service-oriented rather than flashy luxury.
- Styling: Business-casual or smart casual. Avoid over-styled fashion, wedding portrait cues, or obvious template-stock poses.

## What To Avoid
- Western suburb signals such as front lawns, detached-cookie-cutter homes, or English-only “for sale by owner” boards.
- Glossy CGI or 3D-rendered real-estate visuals unless the section is explicitly about a design tool.
- Overprocessed HDR, fake lens blur, or surreal orange-and-teal grading.
- Smiling-call-center-stock tropes that undermine trust.

## Composition Rules
- Preserve the current aspect ratio and safe crop of the file being replaced.
- For transparent slots, keep a transparent canvas and place the new image inside a shaped frame or floating composition so the surrounding layout still breathes.
- For avatar and testimonial images, maintain center-weighted framing that survives circle and square crops.
- For fallback property images, retain enough negative space for badges, labels, and overlay text.

## Category Briefs
- Hero and locality exteriors: Real apartment towers, builder floors, gated communities, balconies, landscaped entrances, and NCR-style facades in daylight.
- Trust and about sections: Indian buyers or advisors inside modern Indian homes, relaxed expressions, credible wardrobe, and no hard-sell props.
- Owner CTA and support: Indian owner or RM in a consultative moment, ideally delivered as a transparent composition.
- Editorial and gallery: Indian interiors, neighborhoods, home-search moments, and property discovery scenes that match the page topic.
- Avatars and team portraits: Indian professionals with clean backgrounds, moderate contrast, and sharp eye-line framing.

## Processing Standards
- Use `AVIF` or `WebP` for opaque photos when the slot already supports those formats.
- Keep `PNG` for transparency-dependent slots.
- Export at the existing intrinsic dimensions to avoid layout shift.
- Match the site’s warm orange brand accents through surrounding UI, not by tinting the image heavily.
